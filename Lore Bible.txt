MY GAME - LORE BIBLE
Version: 0.3
Status: Working Canon Baseline
Language: English

============================================================
1. CORE PREMISE
============================================================

Working title: My Game

The game is a low-fantasy medieval action RPG with a structure inspired by
hub-based death-loop design. The lore, tone, mythology, and worldbuilding are
intended to be fully original.

The player awakens in an old, decaying fort after being dragged in from the
forest by two guards. The protagonist has no memory of who they are. The fort
serves as the central hub. Each time the player dies, they awaken there again.

Beyond the fort lie multiple hostile regions and bosses. A defining twist is
that whenever the player re-enters a level, it is changed: routes shift,
landmarks move, and the world no longer behaves as if it is fixed.

This instability is caused by a spreading Curse, which is the central force of
the setting and the main driver of the game's lore.


============================================================
2. SETTING AND TONE
============================================================

The world should feel grounded, harsh, and medieval, with restrained magic.
Magic exists, as do magical creatures, but neither should be common or flashy.
The supernatural is real, but it should feel rare, old, and weighty.

A useful tonal comparison is The Witcher rather than high fantasy. The setting
should feel lived-in, weathered, political, and human, while still allowing for
deep mythic undercurrents.

The story should preserve mystery. Not every truth needs to be explained in a
fully literal way, and some elements should remain open to interpretation.
However, the underlying foundations of the world should be internally strong and
coherent.


============================================================
3. THE CURSE - CURRENT CORE CONCEPT
============================================================

The strongest current formulation is:

    It begins with place, reveals itself through memory, and ultimately
    consumes identity.

The Curse is not merely rot, corruption, or madness. It is a failure in the
bond between a thing and its proper meaning.

Effects of the Curse include:
- roads forgetting where they lead
- boundaries losing certainty
- landmarks drifting
- memories becoming contradictory
- names and identities losing their hold

This makes the Curse ideal for the game structure. Regions shift, survivors
return confused, and death itself becomes unstable for certain people touched by
its deeper workings.


============================================================
4. CAUSE OF THE CURSE
============================================================

The current preferred interpretation is that the Curse did not begin as a single
malicious act, nor as a straightforward divine punishment. It began as a long
neglect of the bindings by which the world was once kept coherent.

In this setting, oath, witness, memory, name, rite, and stewardship are not
merely cultural ornaments. They are part of the world's actual order. They help
bind place, lineage, duty, and truth into stable form.

Over generations, these bindings thinned:
- oaths were inherited but no longer fully borne
- rites were preserved in form but not in understanding
- roads were used without being truly known
- titles remained while the burden beneath them weakened

The Curse therefore emerges from vacancy rather than invasion: a long absence
where meaning should have been.

This should not be framed as a simple moral punishment for bad behavior. The
idea is subtler: people came to rely on inherited structure without fully
participating in the truth that once sustained it.


============================================================
5. RELATIONSHIP TO THE GODS
============================================================

The Curse should not be explained as "the gods did this."

A stronger approach is that religion once understood parts of the law that kept
the Curse at bay, but later generations retained the outer forms while losing
the inner weight.

This allows different in-world interpretations:
- some call it divine judgment
- some call it an ancient law failing
- some call it a blight beyond human understanding

None of these interpretations need to be fully confirmed.


============================================================
6. SUBTLE MODERN THEMATIC PARALLEL
============================================================

A quiet modern parallel exists beneath the setting, but it must never be stated
directly or turned into a sermon.

The underlying idea is that people become fragile when they stop engaging with
reality directly and begin relying too heavily on borrowed certainty, inherited
appearances, convenience, reputation, and second-hand truth.

Translated into the world of the game, this means:
- men trusting markers more than memory
- repeating doctrine without understanding
- clinging to titles without bearing their burden
- valuing ceremony over witness
- preserving forms after their living meaning has faded

The tone should remain timeless rather than explicitly contemporary.


============================================================
7. THE FORT
============================================================

The fort is the central hub and one of the last places in the region still able
to remain coherent against the Curse.

Current reason:
This is the great fort of the region where squires of a special knightly order
are knighted after severe trials.

The place is saturated with:
- vow
- memory
- witness
- ordeal
- earned identity

Because these things still have real weight there, the fort remains relatively
stable and uncorrupted.

The fort is not untouched because of a generic magical shield. It endures
because it is one of the few places where meaning was made and bound with
unusual force.


============================================================
8. THE ORDER AND THE OATH
============================================================

The knightly order tied to the fort is central to the region's old structure and
to the player's eventual identity.

The oath is sacred in meaning, martial in tone, and austere in style. It should
feel ancient, hard, and unornamented.

Oath text:

    I take no name above the charge I bear.
    I shall keep watch where others falter.
    I shall keep true what is given into my care: road, boundary, memory,
    and oath.
    I shall not claim dominion where I am sworn only to keep.
    I shall not turn from duty for fear, want, love, or pride.
    If I fail, let my name be broken before my word.
    So long as I stand, let this place remain true.


============================================================
9. WHY THE PLAYER RETURNS TO THE FORT
============================================================

A major current direction is that the player once took this oath in the fort,
even though they no longer remember it at the start of the game.

This creates the lore basis for the death loop. The protagonist does not simply
respawn because of a gameplay abstraction; they return because the fort is a
place that still recognizes and binds them.

The player was first found in the forest and physically brought back into the
fort by two guards. That matters as well. The fort becomes both:
- the site of the old oath
- the first place in the present where the player is witnessed, received, and
  re-established

Preferred interpretation:
The protagonist has become metaphysically unresolved through contact with the
deeper workings of the Curse. The world cannot fully settle them into death, so
they are drawn back to the last place that still holds them in coherent form.


============================================================
10. THE PROTAGONIST AT THE START
============================================================

The protagonist is found in the forest, brought into the ruined fort, and
awakened without memory.

They can still speak coherently, which makes them unusual. Others who return
from deeper contact with the Curse are often confused, shattered, or unable to
give any clear account.

This creates immediate suspicion and hope among the fort's inhabitants. Some
view the stranger as a threat, others as a possible answer, and many simply do
not know what to make of them.

The protagonist is magically inclined in some fashion, but magic in the world
should remain restrained rather than common or spectacular.


============================================================
11. THE KNIGHT-LORD
============================================================

The Knight-Lord is a key figure in the lore and should remain central to Level 1,
but he is not the cause of the Curse.

The Curse had long plagued the wider land from afar. Only when it drew closer to
the fort did he begin to investigate it directly.

He was a hard, proven, imposing man: not necessarily beloved, but formidable and
effective. He held the lordship longer than any knight before him, and over time
that endurance fed a fatal sense of personal claim.

He did not openly reject the oath. On the contrary, he was proud of it, had
suffered to earn it, and likely believed himself its truest living bearer.

His tragedy is that fear changed the spirit of his rule by degrees. As the
Curse neared, he became increasingly controlling, possessive, and fearful of
losing authority, all while convincing himself that he was preserving the fort.


============================================================
12. HOW THE KNIGHT-LORD FAILED
============================================================

It is important that his failure was gradual, not reducible to one single act
that caused everything.

This matches the nature of the Curse itself: a thinning, an erosion, a slow
replacement of living meaning with hardened form.

The Knight-Lord began governing as though possession could protect what
stewardship could not. He increasingly treated the keep as something that
belonged to him by right of endurance.

One especially strong established element:
He would not alter the oath itself, but he began refusing knighthood to capable,
independent squires who questioned his authority.

He feared that strong-minded young men might weaken his control, divide the
order, or prove unfit to face an unknown and growing horror. In practice, this
meant he confused worthiness with obedience.

As a result, the order remained outwardly intact while slowly hollowing from
within. The best successors were withheld, and the living succession of the
charge weakened.

This made him a local embodiment of the age's deeper problem: preserving form
while betraying truth.


============================================================
13. LEVEL 1 FUNCTION
============================================================

Level 1 should primarily revolve around the Knight-Lord and the player's first
real understanding of the Curse.

Thematically, the Knight-Lord should function less as the source of evil and
more as the first human shape of the world's failure.

He becomes compelling if he is tragic rather than cartoonishly villainous: a man
trying to hold the line, but doing so in the wrong spirit, until fear, pride,
and claim turn him into the very kind of fracture the Curse feeds upon.


============================================================
14. WHAT DOES NOT NEED TO BE OVERDEVELOPED YET
============================================================

The rejected squires do not need to become a major faction or subplot at this
stage.

It is enough that this pattern existed and may surface in fragments:
- a bitter villager
- a drunk
- an old grievance
- a forgotten roll
- a small item description
- a passing line of dialogue

The focus should remain on:
- the Knight-Lord
- the Curse
- the fort
- the oath
- the player's return loop


============================================================
15. RECOMMENDED LORE DELIVERY STYLE
============================================================

The game should communicate its truths through fragments rather than exposition
dumps.

Useful vehicles include:
- item descriptions
- inscriptions
- liturgy
- knighting records
- hearsay
- survivor testimony
- architecture
- offhand NPC dialogue
- environmental contradiction

The player should be able to grasp strong patterns without the setting ever
flattening itself into a lecture.


============================================================
16. USEFUL WORKING PHRASES AND CANON LINES
============================================================

- It begins with place, reveals itself through memory, and ultimately consumes
  identity.
- The Curse did not begin as an invading force. It began as a vacancy.
- Truth must be encountered, not merely received.
- A thing only truly binds you if you have stood in it yourself.
- The Knight-Lord did not cause the Curse. But when it neared the keep, he
  answered it with fear disguised as authority.
- The Curse drew near from without, but he made room for it within.
- Objects that have been held with purpose across generations resist the Curse.
  They remember what they were made for, even when everything else has forgotten.


============================================================
17. ESTABLISHED NPCS — CANON BASELINE
============================================================

EDRIC — The Blacksmith
  Edric was the fort's armorer under the Knight-Lord. When the order began to
  fracture and the Curse crept closer, he stayed working — he believed a good
  blade was still worth something in a falling world. When the fort was finally
  abandoned, he attempted to leave with his tools. He was ambushed and caged
  by the Iron Warden's bandits in the forest.

  He is gruff, pragmatic, and not given to gratitude. He owes the player work,
  not warmth. He does not discuss the Curse at length, but his ambient lines
  suggest he feels it in the metal — the way things corrode that should not,
  the way good iron feels different than it did.

  Edric's Chain:
  A neck-chain passed through every smith in his family line. It predates the
  Curse and has survived intact precisely because it has been held with
  consistent purpose across generations. In a world where meaning is being
  stripped from things, this object still knows what it is.
  Item description: "Worn by every smith in Edric's line. The metal has no
  name, but it has not forgotten its purpose."
  Effect: -15% physical damage received. Introduces physical as the first
  named damage type — others (fire, magic, poison) follow in later phases.

THE IRON WARDEN
  A bandit captain who has claimed a stretch of the forest as his own. His
  title is self-appointed. He carries the key to Edric's cage as a symbol of
  ownership. He is not cursed in any deep sense — just a man who fills a
  vacancy of authority with violence. He is, in a small way, an echo of the
  Knight-Lord: someone who confused possession with stewardship.

CORVIN — The Scavenger Smith (Level 2)
  A self-taught smith who learned by necessity in the margins of a falling
  world. If Edric is alive, Corvin arrives at the hub as a traveling merchant.
  If Edric is dead, Corvin takes over the forge — at higher prices and with
  less skill. He does not ask where things come from. He is not malicious, only
  opportunistic. If Edric is dead, Corvin's presence in the forge role signals a subtle darkening of
  the hub and fortress. If Edric is alive, perhaps Corvin will serve a different role later on which has not been determined yet.
